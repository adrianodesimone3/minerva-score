How the model sees your data

The model rearranges your answers into this small picture and looks at the pattern, the same way it learned from past patients. It's there for transparency — you don't need to read it.

What this means
  • This is a statistical estimate, not a diagnosis. The model sometimes flags people who do fine and misses people who don't — about 4 in 10 of its flags turn out to be readmissions in our test data.
  • If your estimate is in the orange or red zone, talk to your doctor about follow-up — not because something is definitely wrong, but because closer monitoring may be helpful.
  • If it's in the green zone, that aligns with "lower than average risk" in our data — but still follow your clinical team's instructions.

Everything runs in your browser. Your answers are not sent anywhere and nothing is stored.
